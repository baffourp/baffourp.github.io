About

I am a computational chemist specializing in quantum chemistry, excited-state dynamics, and spectroscopic simulations. Applied DFT/TD-DFT, CC/EOM-CC, AIMD, and relativistic methods to catalysis, photophysics, and electrospray propulsion challenges. Designed and validated models that reproduce experimental spectra (e.g., XAS/XCD, UV–Vis) and guided molecular design for CO2 photoreduction, OLED emitters, and metalloporphyrins. 18 peer-reviewed publications, multiple fellowships/awards; experienced collaborator with experimental teams; proficient across Q-CHEM, CFOUR, ORCA, Gaussian, and Python workflows.
I hold certification in molecular modeling for Drug Discovery and possess experience in Free Energy Perturbation.
Outside of research, I’m interested in philosophy, spending time in nature, and cars. These interests shape how I think about problem-solving, perspective, and design—both inside and outside the lab.
